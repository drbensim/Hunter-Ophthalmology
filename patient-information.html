Who can refer you to Hunter Ophthalmology?

Your family doctor, another specialist or optometrist are able to refer you.

A referral from your GP or optometrist is valid for 12 months.

A referral from your specialists is valid for 3 months.

Items to bring for your initial appointment.

  • Your referral
  • Your current distance or reading glasses
  • Current list of medications and allergies
  • Medicare Card
  • If applicable, your Pension Card / DVA Card / Health Fund Card

What to expect on your arrival at Hunter Ophthalmology?

Upon your arrival, you will be checked in by our administrative team. We will provide you a questionnaire to fill prior to being screened by our ophthalmic technician.

Additional tests may be performed and your eyes may be dilated during this period. You will then be seen by the doctor.

How long should I expect to spend at your consultation?

At Hunter Ophthalmology, we respect and value time and strive to not rush our consultations, giving you the time you deserve for a thorough eye examination and to address any questions.

Please allow approximately 1–2 hours for your initial appointment, noting that this may take longer if additional tests are needed.

Can you drive after your appointment?

Dilating drops may be administered for a more comprehensive eye assessment. This will cause blurred vision for about 4 hours, and you should avoid driving during that time.

Should I bring someone else to my appointment?

We strongly advise that someone accompanies you and is available to take you home safely. If that isn’t possible, please arrange alternative transportation.
